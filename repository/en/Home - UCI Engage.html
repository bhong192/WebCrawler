About UCI Engage

UCI Engage embodies our campus commitment to build an inclusive culture of engagement and service and aims to support, amplify and celebrate the curricular and cocurricular engagement efforts of our students both on campus and in the community.

It serves to evaluate and improve the availability and quality of engagement opportunities for undergraduate and graduate students and foster collaborative, empowering and transformative activities that will enhance the student experience at UCI and prepare students for life as citizens and community members beyond UCI.  

Meet the Director

UCI Engage is directed by Dr. Nancy Guerra, Professor in the department of Psychological Science and Dean of the School of Social Ecology. Dr. Guerra’s research focuses on preventing youth violence and promoting healthy youth development. She is an expert on community participatory research and conducts research in the community locally, nationally and internationally. 

Community Engagement Resources for Students

Global Service Scholars Program

UCI is preparing aspiring leaders across the University of California system to solve the problems of tomorrow by offering them an experiential education through the school’s flagship Global Service Scholars program. Learn more at globalservicescholars.uci.edu

Field Study

More than 40 years ago, Social Ecology helped pioneer experiential learning by requiring undergraduates to complete at least 100 hours of field-based learning outside the classroom. Today, the School’s Field Study program pushes students to apply classroom-based learning to real-world problem-solving. Learn more at fieldstudy.soceco.uci.edu
